Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about the Health Risk Management System

General System Information

What is the Health Risk Management System?
The Health Risk Management System is a comprehensive platform that evaluates your potential health risks based on personal health parameters and helps you find suitable hospitals that accept your insurance policy. It also allows you to compare different insurance policies and generates detailed health reports for your reference.
How does the risk score evaluation work?
The risk score evaluation uses machine learning algorithms trained on both Kaggle datasets and custom-collected data. It analyzes the information you provide about your lifestyle habits, medical history, and family chronic disease history to calculate a risk score that indicates your potential for developing certain health issues.
What parameters are used to calculate my risk score?
Three main parameters are used:
  • Lifestyle habits (such as diet, exercise, smoking, alcohol consumption)
  • Medical history (past illnesses, surgeries, current conditions)
  • Family chronic disease history (hereditary conditions and diseases among close relatives)
How accurate is the risk score prediction?
The risk score is based on statistical models trained on extensive datasets. While it provides a good indication of potential health risks, it should be used as a screening tool rather than a definitive medical diagnosis. Always consult healthcare professionals for proper medical advice.

Data and Technology

What datasets were used to develop the risk assessment model?
The model was trained using:
  • Public healthcare datasets from Kaggle
  • Custom datasets collected through Google Forms surveys
These datasets contain anonymized health information that helps the system identify patterns and correlations between lifestyle factors and health outcomes.
How does the hospital visualization feature work?
The system uses Tableau, a business intelligence tool, to visualize hospitals in your residing city that accept your insurance policy. The visualization displays relevant information such as hospital locations, specialties, ratings, and covered services under your policy.
What BI tools are used for hospital visualization?
Tableau is the primary business intelligence tool used for visualizing hospital data and insurance coverage information. It processes custom datasets uniquely fetched for each city in association with the presence of insurance policy companies.
How is the custom dataset for hospital information updated?
The hospital dataset is custom-built and regularly updated by collecting information directly from hospitals and insurance providers. This ensures that you receive the most current information about hospital services and insurance coverage.

Features and Usage

Can I view hospitals that accept my insurance policy?
Yes, after inputting your insurance policy provider, the system will display a visualization of hospitals in your city that accept your policy. This helps you make informed decisions during emergencies or when planning medical care.
Can I compare different insurance policies?
Yes, the system allows you to compare different insurance companies based on various aspects according to your specific needs, such as coverage types, premium costs, hospital networks, and claim settlement ratios.
What information is included in the generated reports?
The reports include:
  • Your risk score and its interpretation
  • Breakdown of risk factors based on your lifestyle, medical history, and family history
  • List of hospitals accepting your insurance policy
  • Recommendations for health improvements
  • Comparative analysis of insurance policies (if requested)
How can I access my previous risk assessments?
Your risk assessment history is stored in your user profile. You can access previous assessments by logging into your account and navigating to the "Assessment History" section.

Health and Privacy Concerns

Is my medical data kept private and secure?
Yes, all personal and medical information is encrypted and stored securely following healthcare data protection standards. Your data is never shared with third parties without your explicit consent.
How often should I update my risk assessment?
It's recommended to update your risk assessment annually or whenever significant changes occur in your health status, lifestyle habits, or family health history.
Can the system predict specific health conditions?
The system identifies potential risk areas rather than diagnosing specific conditions. It highlights categories of health concerns you may be susceptible to based on your input data, but does not provide medical diagnoses.
What should I do if my risk score is high?
If your risk score is high, consider:
  • Consulting with a healthcare professional
  • Reviewing the specific risk factors highlighted in your report
  • Making recommended lifestyle modifications
  • Setting up regular health screenings for early detection of potential issues
How does family chronic disease history impact my risk score?
Family health history significantly influences your risk score as many conditions have genetic components. The system analyzes patterns of diseases in your family to identify hereditary risks that might affect you.
Are lifestyle recommendations provided based on my risk score?
Yes, the system provides personalized lifestyle recommendations based on your risk assessment. These may include suggestions for dietary changes, exercise routines, preventive screenings, and other health-promoting behaviors.

Technical Questions

Can I share my risk assessment report with my doctor?
Yes, reports are designed to be easily shared with healthcare providers. You can download your report as a PDF or directly share it through the system's secure sharing feature.
Is the system available as a mobile application?
Yes, the Health Risk Management System is available as both a web application and a mobile app for iOS and Android devices, allowing you to access your health information on the go.
